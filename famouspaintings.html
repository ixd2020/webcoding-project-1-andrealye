THE PERSISTENCE OF MEMORY

The Persistence of Memory (1931)
This painting depicts clocks melting on a deserted landscape. The contrasting use of hard and soft represents the realities of the world. It can be interpreted like the human mind moving from the softness of sleep to the hardness of reality. The melting clocks are an unconscious symbol of the relativity of space and time, especially when we are dreaming. The ants symbolise decay, with how we keep track of time in our daily lives. The Persistence of Memory is considered one of the greatest masterpieces of twentieth century art. It is not only the most famous painting of Salvador Dali but also the most renowned artwork in Surrealism.

Swans Reflecting Elephants (1937)
